# ADAMTS5 in Knee Osteoarthritis

## Gene Overview

ADAMTS5 (A Disintegrin and Metalloproteinase with Thrombospondin motifs 5), also known as aggrecanase-2, is encoded by the *ADAMTS5* gene on human chromosome 21q21.3. ADAMTS5 is a secreted zinc metalloproteinase that cleaves aggrecan — the core proteoglycan of articular cartilage — at specific glutamyl-alanine bonds. Unlike the matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs), ADAMTS5 is highly substrate-specific and primarily targets aggrecan rather than fibrillar collagens.

## Role in Osteoarthritis

Aggrecan loss is the earliest detectable biochemical event in osteoarthritic cartilage, preceding collagen degradation. By cleaving aggrecan at the Glu373-Ala374 site, ADAMTS5 exposes cartilage to mechanical injury and further enzymatic degradation. In the classical model of OA pathogenesis, aggrecanase-mediated aggrecan loss is a reversible early event, while subsequent MMP13-mediated type II collagen cleavage drives irreversible structural damage.

ADAMTS5 is induced in OA chondrocytes by pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1, TNF-α) and mechanical overload. Both ADAMTS4 and ADAMTS5 act as aggrecanases in human cartilage, but mouse studies consistently demonstrate that ADAMTS5 is the dominant aggrecanase driving OA progression.

## Key Evidence

- **ADAMTS5 knockout mice are protected from surgical OA:** In the classic Glasson et al. (2005) study, cartilage-specific *Adamts5⁻/⁻* mice showed dramatic protection against OA after DMM surgery, while *Adamts4⁻/⁻* mice showed no protection (PMID 15800624). This pair of studies established ADAMTS5 as the primary pathogenic aggrecanase in mice.
- **Human aggrecanase activity:** In human articular cartilage, both ADAMTS4 and ADAMTS5 contribute to aggrecanolysis. In vitro studies using cytokine-stimulated human cartilage explants show ADAMTS5 accounts for the majority of aggrecanase activity.
- **Aggrecan neoepitope NITEGE:** The ADAMTS-specific aggrecan cleavage fragment (NITEGE neoepitope) is detectable in OA synovial fluid and serves as a biomarker for disease activity.
- **Gene expression:** Single-cell and bulk RNA-seq studies consistently show upregulation of *ADAMTS5* in OA chondrocytes, particularly in pre-hypertrophic and hypertrophic chondrocyte subpopulations.

## Clinical Relevance

Several ADAMTS5 inhibitor programs have advanced to clinical development:

- **GLPG1972 / S201086 (Galapagos / Servier):** An orally active small-molecule ADAMTS5 inhibitor that advanced to Phase II (ROCCELLA trial). The trial failed to meet its primary endpoint of cartilage thickness change at 52 weeks, but exploratory analyses suggested biomarker engagement.
- **Anti-ADAMTS5 monoclonal antibodies:** Several biotech programs have pursued antibody-based ADAMTS5 inhibition, with the goal of achieving greater selectivity and longer half-life than small molecules.
- **siRNA and intra-articular delivery approaches:** Emerging modalities seek to combine sustained cartilage residence with aggrecanase-specific knockdown.

The ROCCELLA failure raised important questions:
1. Whether aggrecanase inhibition alone is sufficient to slow OA progression in humans
2. Whether patient stratification (e.g., early-stage OA, aggrecan-loss-dominant subtype) would improve outcomes
3. Whether combination therapy (ADAMTS5 + MMP13, or ADAMTS5 + anabolic factor) is required

## Summary

ADAMTS5 is the dominant aggrecanase in cartilage and a strongly validated target for osteoarthritis based on mouse genetics. Despite preclinical validation, clinical translation has been challenging, suggesting that aggrecanase inhibition may require patient stratification or combination approaches to demonstrate disease modification.
